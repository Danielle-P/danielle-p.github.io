2007 - 2011

Law Degree & Legal Practice Course

 University of Leeds  BPP University

I attended the University of Leeds from 2007 to 2010, attaining a 2:1 Law LLB degree and ranking 17th in my cohort of over 220 students.

Following my law degree, I completed the Legal Practice Course (LPC) post-graduate diploma at the BPP University in Leeds where I was awarded a Distinction. Modules I took during the LPC included Business Law and Practice and Intellectual Property Law.

2011 - 2015

Legal Executive

 Squire Patton Boggs  Gordons LLP

After the completion of my Law degree and LPC, I became a Legal Executive in the field of Banking and Financial Services Law. I initially worked for Squire Patton Boggs, a large international law firm, and then moved on to the Yorkshire-based firm Gordons LLP in 2013.

During my time as a Legal Executive I progressed from working as part of a team of seven on banking and financial services transactions to managing my own caseload of corporate property finance transactions.

At both Squire Patton Boggs and Gordons I was part of a team which often had a large workload. I was able to efficiently prioritise my various roles, completing individual tasks to deadlines and also assisting the team with collective projects.

My experience in Banking Law helped me to build on my ability to work in a high pressure environment and use my initiative to handle unanticipated issues calmly and positively.

2015 - 2016

Tech Solutions Graduate Scheme

 KPMG LLP

I joined the KPMG TechSolutions graduate scheme in October 2015. My career change from law into the technology sector was driven by my ambition to work in a less prescriptive and more creative industry with greater opportunity for learning and growth.

My previous qualifications and experience meant I was able to bring to this role, and my future roles in this sector, sophisticated analytical, problem-solving and commercial reasoning skills.

I initially joined the KPMG Learning Academy as an Instructional Designer where I gained valuable experience in designing for learning and was introduced to the principles of design. This role included:

  • Liaising with stakeholders on the subject matter of online courses
  • Writing content for online courses
  • Designing user interactions

2016 - 2018

UX Designer

 KPMG Nunwood

In February 2016 I moved to the KPMG Nunwood team to train as a UX Designer, working with the team that designs and builds the Customer Experience Cloud (CX Cloud) product. In this role I was able to rapidly expand my design skills and gained valuable experience in:

  • Designing for data visualisation
  • Micro-interaction design
  • Infographic design
  • Designing mock-ups and interactive wireframes

As a UX designer I also trained in front-end development, including:

  • HTML and Bootstrap
  • CSS (primarily using LESS)
  • jQuery and basic JavaScript
  • Using razor templates, C# and the Highcharts library to create data visualisations

2018 - present

Senior UX Designer

 KPMG Nunwood

I was promoted to Senior UX Designer in 2018. My role entailed acting as the UX Lead and front-end developer on client projects alongside contributing to the design of elements of the CX Cloud product.

I worked with product and engineering teams to design the UX and UI of features, new components and modules for our core dashboard product. The product itself is highly customisable and I was responsible for the UX design and the front-end build of bespoke implementations of the product for our clients. Client implementations include household-name brands in the energy, airline and financial sectors. My key responsibilities included:

  • Being the UX client contact on projects throughout the design and build phases as well as during the BAU phase
  • Requirements gathering from clients at scoping sessions
  • Creating initial designs through to interactive wireframes for client dashboard solutions
  • Building the front-end and user interactions for client dashboards
  • Delegating and coordinating front-end build tasks

During my time at KPMG Nunwood I designed and built a number of long-term, successful client dashboards which are used by up to 40,000 users each month.

2020 - present

Product & UX / UI Designer

 GreenPowerMonitor, DNV GL

Having moved to Barcelona in August 2020, I started my current role as a Product and UX / UI designer for a market-leading monitoring platform for the renewable energy sector.

As the designer for 3 teams of developers, I work closely with product owners to design the UX, UI and interactions for new product components and features. My key responsibilities include:

  • Working with product owners to define user stories
  • Requirements gathering from stakeholders
  • Creating initial designs through to polished prototypes
  • Testing assumptions, articulating design decisions and incorporating feedback into final designs
  • Handing over designs to engineers and being the point of contact during implementation phase
  • Conducting heuristic reviews of existing components within the platform
